Our Mission

The Commons is a community conspiring to proclaim the cross, working to cultivate disciples, embracing the oppressed, and inviting you to join the movement.

Our History

The Commons (formerly Hope Church North Park) began in 2011 with just a few people meeting in a living room. We were passionate about Jesus and passionate about seeing local, kingdom oriented, churches planted. So, with the help and support of another San Diego church that shared our vision, we began meeting publicly in 2012. Since then, we have continued to grow by establishing Elders and baptizing new disciples as we persist in serving our community and inviting all to join God's movement.

Why are we called The Commons?

It was a mark of the church for people to hold their lives and their possessions in common. We read one examples of this in the book of Acts. There is even this tradition within the history of the Church for naming something for all people, a "common." (Common Book of Prayer, Common Bible, Common Text of the Church)

Therefore, we wanted to select a name for our community that not only spoke to our mission, our identity in Christ, but also one that had historical significance. Church)

For something to be "common" was not to be ordinary, but to belong to the people. For something to be a "commons" was to represent a common space or resource that was of the people. Church)

Now, our culture has lost the language and meaning of a "commons." Physical space and common resources are increasingly quantified and commercialized. We might not call them "commons", yet, parks, beaches, playgrounds, public utilities and public transportation systems, libraries, museums, and schools are all "commons." Church)

The church is a commons. The church is not a private enterprise, but a public work. The church is the Body of Christ, its work is to witness to the Cross, and its worship is the work of the people. The church is not a private good to be packaged and sold. The church is not a commodity, or institution. The church is collective, a band of natural enemies not held together not by common race, education, income, or politics. The church is a band of natural enemies saved by Jesus, owing Jesus a common allegiance. Church)

Christ, his broken body and shed blood, is at the center of all we do together. We gather because we hold a common allegiance to Christ. We gather for the common work of making Christ, His Cross, and His Kingdom visible to all. Church)

We are The Commons. The Commons for all.
